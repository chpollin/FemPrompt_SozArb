## Relevant Concepts

**Participatory Design:** Methodologies that actively involve affected communities in designing solutions, policies, or technologies that impact them, rather than imposing top-down solutions.

**Intersectionality:** Framework recognizing that individuals hold multiple, overlapping identities (race, gender, sexuality, class) that interact to create distinct experiences of oppression and privilege.

**Community-Led Activism:** Grassroots organizing where affected communities lead decision-making, strategy, and implementation rather than external experts or institutions.

**Algorithmic Harm:** Negative impacts caused by AI systems, including discrimination, privacy violations, misrepresentation, and identity erasure affecting specific communities.

**Decentralized Governance:** Organizational structures distributing decision-making power across members rather than concentrating authority in hierarchical leadership.

**Operationalization:** The process of translating theoretical principles or commitments into concrete, measurable practices and policies.

**Marginalized Communities:** Groups systematically excluded from power, resources, and decision-making due to social hierarchies based on identity characteristics.
