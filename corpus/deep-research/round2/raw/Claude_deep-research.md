AB  - Using intersectionality theory (Crenshaw; Collins' matrix of domination), the authors analyse 500,000 observations from five publicly available language models (ChatGPT 3.5, ChatGPT 4, Claude 2.0, Llama 2, PaLM 2) generated through open-ended ("laissez-faire") prompting rather than explicit identity prompts. They find that generated narratives systematically omit characters with minoritized race, gender and sexual-orientation identities relative to U.S. Census levels, relegate them to subordinated rather than dominant roles, and reproduce stereotyping. The study demonstrates that discrimination is co-constituted across multiple identity axes and surfaces even when identity terms are not specified, making the case that everyday prompting practices can render intersectional harm visible. The authors conclude that individual mitigation is insufficient and call for regulation to ensure responsible innovation and protect consumers.
