# IMPROVED DETAILED SUMMARY: Incubating Feminist AI 2021-2024

## Overview
The Feminist AI Research Network (f<a+i>) addresses a critical gap between theoretical feminist critique of AI systems and practical implementation of equitable, inclusive technologies. Existing AI development perpetuates historical exclusions and gender-based inequities. This initiative operationalizes "Feminist AI"—systems designed with inclusion at their core to deliver equality outcomes—across 10 countries in Latin America, MENA, and Southeast Asia. The core thesis: technology can advance gender and social justice when developed through multidisciplinary collaboration, community co-creation, and human rights frameworks that move beyond harm prevention toward creating new opportunities for marginalized communities.

## Main Findings

1. **Operationalized Feminist AI Framework**: Feminist AI is implementable through structured methodology, expanding beyond preventing gender-based violence (the "floor") to creating proactive opportunities for women and marginalized communities (the "ceiling").

2. **Scalable Prototypes Addressing Real Harms**: Five projects advanced to pilot stage:
   - **SafeHer Transit** (Philippines): Addresses 80% harassment rates for Manila commuters through AI-driven transit safety app with defensive functions and collective action integration
   - **E.D.I.A.** (Argentina): Identifies bias and stereotypes in natural language processing software, evolved from single-word detection to contextual, intersectional analysis
   - **AymurAI** (Argentina): Measures gender-based violence in Latin America through collaboration with feminist NGO Data Género and criminal justice systems
   - **SOF+IA** (Chile): GenAI chatbot addressing digital harassment and violence situations women experience on social media platforms
   - **AI & Equality: A Human Rights Toolbox** (Multi-regional): Course and community resource across three regional hubs

3. **Multidisciplinary Collaboration Essential**: Successful projects combined computer scientists, philosophers, social scientists, NGOs, and affected communities—no single discipline alone could achieve feminist AI outcomes.

4. **Regional Adaptation with Institutional Integration**: Three decentralized hubs (Latin America, MENA, Southeast Asia) maintained unified principles while adapting to local contexts. SafeHer's pilot includes De La Salle University adoption for female students and active cooperation with Manila transit authorities and government units.

5. **Academic-to-Practice Translation Gap**: Research requires diversified dissemination beyond peer-reviewed journals (op-eds, podcasts, webinars, creative essays) to reach policymakers and practitioners.

6. **Intersectional Analysis Strengthens Tools**: E.D.I.A.'s evolution from single-word bias detection to contextual, intersectional analysis (examining intersections like female-male with fat-skinny dimensions) demonstrates how feminist frameworks improve technical capabilities.

## Methodology/Approach

The f<a+i> employed a **Paper → Prototype → Pilot progression** across three regional hubs. Nine standalone papers were funded; four advanced to prototype stage; five reached pilot implementation. Research teams combined technologists and social scientists in multidisciplinary collaboration, grounded in design justice principles and human rights frameworks. Community co-creation was embedded throughout, ensuring affected populations shaped tool development. Teams conducted participatory design processes, simulations with stakeholders (e.g., SafeHer's successful simulations with Manila police), and community training (e.g., E.D.I.A.'s training of 500 Argentine high school teachers). **Feminist AI:** Artificial intelligence systems designed with inclusion as foundational principle, delivering equality outcomes and creating opportunities for proactive correction of historical inequities.

**Design Justice:** Framework centering affected communities' voices in technology design, prioritizing equity and accountability over efficiency or innovation alone.

**Paper → Prototype → Pilot Methodology:** Structured progression from research validation through proof-of-concept development to real-world implementation with stakeholder partnerships.

**Intersectional Analysis:** Examining how multiple identity dimensions (gender, class, ethnicity, ability) interact within systems, moving beyond single-axis bias detection.
